# Quoting Greg Kroah-Hartman

> <blockquote cite="https://www.theregister.com/2026/03/26/greg_kroahhartman_ai_kernel/"><p>Months ago, we were getting what we called 'AI slop,' AI-generated security reports that were obviously wrong or low quality. It was kind of funny. It didn't really worry us.</p>
<p>Something happened a month ago, and the world switched. Now we have real reports. All open source projects have real reports that are made with AI, but they're good, and they're real.</p></blockquote>
<p class="cite">&mdash; <a href="https://www.theregister.com/2026/03/26/greg_kroahhartman_ai_kernel/">Greg Kroah-Hartman</a>, Linux kernel maintainer (<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greg_Kroah-Hartman">bio</a>), in conversation with Steven J. Vaughan-Nichols</p>
